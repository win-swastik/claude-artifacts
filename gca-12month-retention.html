Methodology: For each month M, we identify all unique clients who completed a withdrawal in the prior N months. We then check how many of those same clients also completed a withdrawal in month M. Retention % = retained / prior Nm cohort × 100. The 12-month window naturally has a larger denominator (more clients touched in a year), so the % is lower — but the gap narrowing over time signals that long-dormant clients are re-engaging.
